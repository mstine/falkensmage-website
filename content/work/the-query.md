Ninety minutes, one deck, no love-and-light nonsense. I'm not here to tell you Tuesday's going to be great. I'm an archetypal tarotist — I read the cards the way I read a legacy codebase: for the pattern underneath the surface behavior, the load-bearing assumption nobody questioned, the process that's been running in production since before anyone remembers deploying it.

The cards aren't predicting your life. They're mirrors to the psyche — the unconscious speaking in the only language it has: archetypal images, shadow material, the figures of your inner cast offering themselves to be seen. So we don't decode a future. We read what's already happening — the major arcana as stations on the individuation path, the Tower or the Hermit or the Star, wherever you actually are on the way to yourself, and the suits as the cognitive functions, telling us which register the threshold is calling from.

We'll pull a spread built around whatever's stuck — a decision, a relationship, a version of yourself you're trying to exit — and I'll name what the cards point at, plainly, even when it's not what you hoped for. The shadow cards especially: Death, the Devil, the Tower. We don't dress them up and we don't catastrophize them. We see them. That's the whole job.

If you've been carrying a question for months because you already suspect the answer and don't want to be the one who says it first — you don't have to hold it alone anymore. Bring it here. I'll say it with you.
